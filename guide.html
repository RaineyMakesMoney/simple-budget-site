How to Budget Your Money

Creating a budget doesn’t need to be complicated. The goal is simple: know where your money goes so you can make smarter decisions. Let’s walk through the process step by step.

1. Know Your Income

Write down your total take-home pay — what actually hits your bank account after taxes. Include any side income, tips, or freelance earnings. If your income changes monthly, use an average from the past few months.

2. Track Your Spending

Before you make a budget, you need to see where your money goes now. Look at your last 2–3 months of bank and credit card statements. Categorize expenses into essentials (rent, utilities, groceries) and non-essentials (subscriptions, dining out, shopping).

3. Set Realistic Spending Limits

Once you see your spending patterns, assign limits to each category. Don’t aim for perfection — aim for progress. For example:

  • Housing: 30% of income
  • Food & Groceries: 15%
  • Transportation: 10%
  • Savings & Debt Repayment: 20%
  • Everything Else: 25%

4. Use a Simple System

Pick a budgeting method that fits your lifestyle:

  • 50/30/20 Rule – 50% needs, 30% wants, 20% savings.
  • Zero-based Budget – assign every dollar a job.
  • Cash Envelope System – use physical envelopes to control spending.

5. Review & Adjust Monthly

No budget is perfect on the first try. Review your spending each month and adjust as needed. The goal is steady improvement — not restriction.
